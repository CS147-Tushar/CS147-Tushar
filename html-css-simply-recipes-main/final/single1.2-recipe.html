Onion Pakoda Recipe

Make Onion Pakoda that are delictely light, crunchy & crispy with a hint fo natural sweetness and spiciness in every bite. Also known as Onion Pakoda, these super addicive and delicious tea time Snacks bites are made with gram flour, spices, herbs and loads of onions. A big bowl of crunchy onion pakoda, chutney, some piping hot Masala chai on the side, raindrops and soft music playing in the background. you reach food nirvana! This is the magic of humble pakoda.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

How to make Onion Pakoda

To a mixing bowl, add the following ingredients

  1. 2 cups sliced onions (moderately thin)
  2. 1/2 teaspoon minced ginger (or fine chopped)
  3. 2 to 3 chopped green chilies
  4. 10 chopped mint leaves(or 2 tablespoons coriander leaves)
  5. 1 sping curry leaves(chopped)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon salt

step 1

Avoid slicing the onions too thin or too thick. They should be moderately thin. Check video to know how thin.

step 2

Mix all of them will and squeeze the onions a couple of times so they begin to release moisture. Set this aside for 5 to 10 minutes.

step 3

Sprinkle evenly the following

  1. 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  2. 1 1/4 cup besan(gram flour)
  3. 1 1/2 tablespoon rice flour (or cornstarch or semolina)
  4. 1/ teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds, vaamu)
  5. 1/4 teaspoon salt
  6. 1/4 cup cashews (optinal, soaked for 15 mins.) You can also add half teaspoon garam masala.

Make Dough

step 4

Mix everything well. You get a dry mixture now.

step 5

Sprinkle 2 tablespoons water each time and mix the flour to make a moist dough. Do not Pour water, just sprinkle to get the dough right. Sprinkle more water as needed.

I use little over 1/4 cup, but it depends on the amount of moisture your onions release. The mixture must be tight but not too dry. It should not be soggy or dripping otherwise they soak up lot of oil and will not crispy. Taste test this and add more salt if needed.

Heat 1 tablespoons oil in a kadai until very hot. When it turns hot pour it to the dough. Gently mix with a spoon and not hand as it will be very hot. You can also skip this step.

Fry Onion Pakoda

step 6

Next heat oil in the same pan for deep frying until medium hot. Check if it is hot enough by dropping a small quantity of dough in the oil, it must rise without browning and not sink.

This is the right time to begin frying. Take small portions and gently drop them in the oil. Fry on a medium heat stirring heat stirring occasionally for even frying.

step 7

Fry the onion pakoda till golden and crisp. Lastly remove them to a colander of kitchen tissue. To fry the next batch ensure the oil is not very hot. Reduce the flame slightly and fry them.

Serve onion pakoda hot with a cup to tea or coffee. Sprinkle chaat masala if desired.
